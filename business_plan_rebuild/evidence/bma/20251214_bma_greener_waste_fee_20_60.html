How to separate waste according to No-Mix Waste at Home Campaign

Proper waste separation reduces the impact on the environment and increases the chances of recycling. The main types of waste are: Food waste (Put in a green bag/green bin or send for fertilizer and animal feed), Recyclable waste (such as plastic bottles, paper, separate them at collection points or drop them off at the Bangkok garbage trucks), Hazardous waste (such as batteries, light bulbs should be disposed of in the orange bin or at a hazardous waste collection activity in the community), and General waste (such as snack bags, plastic bags, throw them in the blue bin).

Frequently asked questions

1. Why does Bangkok need to adjust the garbage fee?

  • To comply with increasing waste management costs
  • Encourage people to help reduce and separate waste
  • Use the "Pay-As-You-Throw" (PAYT) system

2. What are the new fee rates?

  • Group 1: Waste ≤ 20 liters/day
    • No waste separation → Pay 60 baht/month
    • Waste separation → Pay 20 baht/month
  • Group 2: Waste 20 liters - 1 cubic meter/day → 120 baht/month/20 liters
  • Group 3: Waste > 1 cubic meter/day → 8,000 baht/month/cubic meter

3. How can we reduce the garbage fee?

  • Register via the BKK Waste Pay app or at the district office
  • Submit evidence of waste separation into 4 types (organic, recycled, hazardous, general)
  • Random checks every 3 months. If you fail 2 times in a row, you must pay the normal 60 baht

4. How to calculate the amount of waste?

  • 0.2 kg = 1 liter
  • Calculate from the number of trash cans or have the district office help estimate

5. What is the difference between individual and group registration?

  • Single type: General residential house, individual registration
  • Group type: housing estate, condo, community, register together

6. What additional services will Bangkok provide from the fee increase?

  • Adding food waste & hazardous waste collection vehicles
  • Collect general waste at least twice a week
  • Provide information to the public on how to properly separate waste

7. What can we do with the separated food waste?

  • Make compost
  • Biogas production
  • Processed into animal feed

8. Where can I dispose of large items of garbage (furniture, mattresses)?

  • Free large waste collection service every Saturday and Sunday
  • Dispose of at the points specified by the district office

9. If paying through a legal entity, what should I do?

Wait for the juristic person to ask for advice from the district office to agree on the registration process, send evidence and receive rights, but keep evidence of waste separation first

10. How can I send evidence through the Bangkok Magic Hands Association?

Check the list of authorized magic partners from the Bangkok Environment Department, such as recycling waste trading platforms / waste donation points for various projects

11. How does Bangkok know which houses actually separate their waste?

  • Photo evidence system in BKK Waste Pay (after August 2025)
  • Evidence collected during field visits by Bangkok officials
  • Use evidence of selling waste or donating waste to various projects of the Bangkok Magic Hands Association
BKK Waste Pay will review the evidence and approve the fee reduction rights
